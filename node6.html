Various Storages

Storages are integral parts of computers. They enable computers to carry information over time as transmission devices carry information over space. In normal cases, a computer system includes several types of storages for different purposes.

CPU Registers
are used as workspace for computation.
Main Memory
is usually volatile but randomly accessible.
Hard Disks
provide computers with persistence and capacity.
Backup Devices
are slow and sequential but have huge capacity.
Caches
bridge the performance and cost gaps between devices.
Networks
are not storage devices but enable distributed storages.
